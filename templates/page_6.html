Introduction

Hello, my name is Jeffrey Reagan. I am an industrial automation professional, here to share insights into my journey with a particular focus on programming Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs), Human-Machine Interfaces (HMIs), and Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems.

My journey began at Alfred State University, where I pursued and successfully completed an Associate's degree in Electrical Construction and Maintenance. It was here I found a passion for programming and automation.

Transitioning from traditional relay logic systems to modern Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) was a turning point early in my career. PLCs provide unparalleled flexibility and efficiency, simplifying control logic design and enabling rapid adjustments without extensive rewiring.

Along with PLCs, improved methods of monitoring and controlling processes were developed. Human machine Interfaces(HMIs) became common on the production floor. These interfaces communicate with the PLC and display realtime data, alarm history, input/output states, control parameters, and user-friendly representations of the machine.

PLCs integrate seamlessly with other industrial components, enabling efficient communication and coordination between different systems. These industrial computers hold and execute the program that controls the field devices(motors, pumps, solenoids) by interfacing directly with the field devices or indirectly through RIO(remote controllers typically held near field devices), VFDs(Variable Frequency Drives),Servo Drives,soft starts, and any other inputs/outputs conventional relay logic would allow.
